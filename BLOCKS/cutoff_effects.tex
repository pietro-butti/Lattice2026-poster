\begin{block}{Cutoff effects at $O(a^2)$}
  The higher-statistics LW ensembles allow the Symanzik expansion to be
  dissected term by term. With a tree-level improved gauge action the
  $\color{jgr}{\expval{\chi S_{2,c}}}$ contribution drops out, leaving the
  observable and the flow as the only sources of $\order{a^2}$ contamination.

  Forming ratios of definitions measured on the \emph{same} configurations
  cancels $\expval{\chi}_\text{cont}$ and, Symanzik expansion dictates:
  %
  \begin{align*}
    {\chi^\text{Zeuthen}_\text{clover}}/{\chi^\text{Zeuthen}_\text{impr}}
      &= 1 + a^2
        \qty[{\color{jbl}\expval{\chi_2^\text{cl}} - \expval{\chi_2^\text{imp}}}]
      + \order{a^4} \,, \\[0.4em]
    {\chi^\text{Wilson}_\text{impr}}/{\chi^\text{Zeuthen}_\text{impr}}
      &= 1 + a^2
        \qty[{\color{jre}\expval{\chi S_{2,\text{fl}}^{W}}
                       - \expval{\chi S_{2,\text{fl}}^{Z}}}]
      + \order{a^4} \,.
  \end{align*}
  We can access directly this two ratio separately
  %
    \begin{figure}
      \includegraphics[scale=1.]{PLOTS/ratios_cutoff_cl.pdf}
    \end{figure}

  The blue points therefore probe the discretisation of the charge alone, the
  red points that of the flow alone; both must reach $1$ at $a^2 = 0$, and do.
  
  The observable-related cutoff effects are more prominent: at the coarsest 
  spacing the clover charge lies $22\%$ below the improved one, against $10\%$ for the flow. 
  
  A parabola in $a^2$ describes the entire range up to $a^2/t_0 = 0.48$, while a linear fit
  restricted to the finest lattices is already sufficient --- the $a^4$ term is
  a genuine mild correction, not an artefact of over-fitting.

\end{block}
