\begin{block}{Cutoff effects at $\order{a^2}$}
  The higher-statistics LW ensembles let us dissect the Symanzik expansion term by
  term, comparing $\chi$ from Zeuthen versus Wilson flow and from the clover versus
  the improved charge.

  Ratios of definitions measured on the \emph{same} configurations cancel
  $\langle {\chi}_\text{cont} \rangle$ together with the action term $\langle{\chi S_{2,c}}\rangle$.
  Zeuthen flow further sets $\langle{\chi S_{2,\text{fl}}}\rangle = 0$, and the improved
  charge $\langle{\chi_2^\text{impr}}\rangle = 0$, leaving a single term in each ratio:
  %
  \begin{align*}
    {\chi^\text{Zeuthen}_\text{clover}}/{\chi^\text{Zeuthen}_\text{impr}}
      &= 1 + a^2\,\expval{\chi_2^\text{clover}} + \order{a^4} \,, \\[0.4em]
    {\chi^\text{Wilson}_\text{impr}}/{\chi^\text{Zeuthen}_\text{impr}}
      &= 1 + a^2\,\expval{\chi S_{2,\text{fl}}^{W}} + \order{a^4} \,.
  \end{align*}
  %
  The two contributions are thus accessible separately from our data.
  \skipline
  %
  \begin{figure}
    \includegraphics[scale=1.]{PLOTS/ratios_cutoff_cl.pdf}
  \end{figure}
  %
  Blue points probe the discretisation of the charge alone, red points that of the
  flow alone. A parabola in $a^2$ describes the full range up to $a^2/t_0 = 0.48$,
  while a linear fit restricted to the finest lattices already suffices: the $a^4$
  term is a mild genuine correction, not an over-fitting artefact.

  Both ratios reach $1$ at $a^2 = 0$ by construction: only \emph{classical} cutoff
  effects enter here, and Zeuthen flow together with the improved charge removes
  them exactly. What this plot gives is their size. The non-trivial \emph{quantum}
  effects come instead from the action used to generate the configurations. These
  are what the left panel of the continuum-limit block is meant to expose, but the
  limited statistics and the unimproved clover charge leave them buried in the
  statistical error. We plan to resolve them with a set of Wilson-action ensembles
  covering the same range of $a$ with comparable statistics.
\end{block}
